Heat Pump Hot Water Payback in Australia

Short answer: if you're currently on gas, expect a payback of roughly 4–5 years. If you're on an old electric resistive system, it's often faster — commonly 3–4 years — because resistive electric hot water is the least efficient option to begin with.

Heat pump hot water is a different kind of upgrade to solar — it only replaces how you heat water, not your whole electricity bill. That makes it one of the simplest upgrades to evaluate, and often one of the fastest to pay back.

Why it's so efficient

A standard electric hot water system generates heat directly from electricity — roughly 1 unit of electricity in for 1 unit of heat out. A heat pump instead moves existing heat from the surrounding air into your water, the same principle as reverse-cycle air conditioning. That lets it deliver several times more heat energy than the electricity it consumes, which is the entire reason the payback is usually so quick.

What actually changes your answer

  • Your current system — replacing gas removes an appliance and its supply charge entirely; if it's your only gas appliance, disconnecting can save a further ~$350–400/yr not included above
  • State-based rebates — Victoria, NSW, and Queensland all offer additional top-ups on top of the federal STC rebate, which can bring your net cost down further
  • Household hot water usage — a larger household with higher usage sees bigger dollar savings from the same efficiency improvement
  • Whether you already have solar — timing your heat pump to run during the day on existing solar generation can push real-world savings higher than these estimates
