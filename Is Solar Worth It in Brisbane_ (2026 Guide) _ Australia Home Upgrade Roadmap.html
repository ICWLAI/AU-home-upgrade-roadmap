Is Solar Worth It in Brisbane?

Short answer: usually yes, and often faster than most of Australia — Brisbane has one of the highest solar yields in the country, which typically means a payback period of roughly 4–7 years depending on your actual bill, roof, and how much power you use during the day.

That range is wide because the real answer depends entirely on your own numbers — not a generic Brisbane average. Here's what actually drives it, sourced, and a working example so you can see the maths for yourself.

Why Brisbane specifically

Queensland has one of the best solar resource levels of any state. A well-installed Brisbane system typically generates around 4.3–4.5 kWh per kW installed per day on average across the year, based on Bureau of Meteorology sunlight data[1] — noticeably higher than Victoria or Tasmania[2]. More sun means a given system size produces more electricity, which is the single biggest lever on payback speed.

What actually changes your answer

  • How much of your usage happens during the day — solar only saves you money on power you'd otherwise be buying, so daytime usage matters more than total usage
  • Your roof's orientation and shading — north-facing and unshaded roofs generate closer to the average; heavily shaded or west-only roofs generate less
  • Whether you're also considering a battery — a different calculation, and not always the next best move even if solar makes sense (see the roadmap tool below)
